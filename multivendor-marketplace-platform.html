What Is a Multi-Vendor Marketplace?

A multi-vendor marketplace is a virtual marketplace where multiple vendors can sell their goods and services as well as customers can conduct their buying and selling process through the internet. The multi-vendor eCommerce website can provide the opportunity to create a marketplace which can assist you to manage online order, multiple vendors, product management, CRM system, stock management, and so on.

Do You Face Such Kind of Dilemma???

If you have already started a multi-vendor eCommerce business, it is obvious you have been using any multi-vendor eCommerce software. To use other multi-vendor eCommerce systems, you may face several kinds of difficulties such as:

WordPress Plugin:

If you use WordPress Plugin to build your multi-vendor eCommerce marketplace, you may face WordPress parse or syntax error, database connection related problems, internal server error, email sending problems, memory exhausted error and so on.

WooCommerce:

Sometimes using the WooCommerce platform, there seems to be a conflict between plugin and theme because of the built-in out-of-date theme and plugin. Also, the major difficulty of WooCommerce is fixing the product image size.

Magento:

If you build your eCommerce marketplace with Magento, sometimes you may face the fact that your products have disappeared and are not displayed. Also, you have to face some hassle to login into your admin panel after installation.

OpenCart:

Though OpenCart is a free and open-source platform, you may face some issues in this platform like complex upgrading process, lengthy checkout system, and canonization issues for SEO as well as it is very difficult to import the inventory data into your database.

Shopify:

The major problem of using this platform is to get products in your store as well as if you are a newbie, it will be difficult to enhance the traffic of your site. Also, you may face difficulty integrating the ERP and multi-channel with your system when you use Shopify.

BigCommerce:

The major difficulty of the BigCommerce platform is that there is no option to change or customize the canonical URLs as well as limited payment methods. Also, you may not quickly convert the traffic into your customers because there is no CTAs button in the blog.
